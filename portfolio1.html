  • Timespan: One Week
  • Software: IFTTT, Web Programming
  • Skills required: Automation, Communication

Project Info

When thinking about how to my portfolio/blog posts, I tried to figure out which applet would actually make my life easier and add value to my work. I looked through the IFTTT webite and narrowed it down to the "applets" that work with Medium (the website I use to host my blog posts). There were plenty of applets that allow you to link the publishing of an article to an action. This includes things like automatically saving the post to google drive, github, posting on LinkedIn, etc. However, I found that the applet allowing me to automatically post a link to twitter made the most sense for my personal needs. Because of the professional nature of LinkedIn, I am wary about posting too much unwanted content. For me, twitter was the perfect place to share my thoughts on automation for anybody who would like to read them.

The process of setting up the automation is quite easy because of the front-end UI from IFTTT. After choosing the applet I wanted to use, the website asked me to sign into Medium and Twitter. This allowed IFTTT to post for me and get profile information from both sites. Now, if I publish anything on Medium, the link will also be shared through twitter.

My main takeaways from this process are how easy automation can be and how useful internet-connected information is. While creating custom automation through the internet of things might still be quite challenging, it's very cool to see how IFTTT is creating a library of pre-built automation applets for anybody to use.
